My Role Model Dr. A.P.J. Abdul kalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was born on 15th October 1931. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the Missile Man, he worked on many important defense projects. He always motivated youth to dream big and work hard. He lived a simple life and was deeply loved by the people.

Kalam played a major role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests. He studied physics and aerospace engineering. He worked with ISRO and DRDO on satellite launch projects. Kalam loved teaching and always met with students. His speeches inspired millions to achieve their goals.

He believed that dreams are not what you see in sleep. Dreams are something that do not let you sleep. He has written many inspirational books for youth. One of his famous books is "Wings of Fire". His thoughts still guide millions of students.

Kalam was honored with Bharat Ratna, Padma Vibhushan, and Padma Bhushan. He always promoted education and innovation. He believed that knowledge with humility is true power. Kalam also worked on India's satellite and missile programs. He remained a teacher at heart till his last breath.

Dr. Kalam passed away on 27th July 2015 while giving a lecture. His death was mourned across the world. Even after his death, his teachings remain alive. People remember him as a true servant of the nation. His life will continue to inspire generations to come.
