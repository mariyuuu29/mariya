My Role Model- Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1. Introduction

	Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was a scientist, teacher,
	and the 11th President of India. 
	He is known as the Missile Man of India 
	and is loved for his simple lifestyle and great thoughts.

2. Early Life

	He was born on 15th October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. 
	His family was financially poor, but he was rich in dreams and determination.

3. Education

	Kalam studied physics and aerospace engineering. 
	He graduated from Madras Institute of Technology. 

	His hard work and love for learning made him successful.
4. Scientific Career
	He joined ISRO and DRDO and worked on India’s missile programs. 
	He played a key role in developing Agni and Prithvi missiles.
5. Nuclear Tests
	In 1998, he helped conduct nuclear tests in Pokhran. 
	This made India a nuclear power and increased respect worldwide.

6. As President

	He became the 11th President of India in 2002. 

	He was known as the People’s President because he loved the youth and students.

7. Love for Students

	Kalam always believed that students are the future of the country. 
	He visited many schools and colleges to inspire young minds.

8. Books Written

	He wrote many books like Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds. 

	These books teach us about dreams, goals, and dedication.
9. Simplicity and Values
	Even after becoming President, he lived a simple life. 

	He believed in honesty, discipline, and hard work.
10. Conclusion
	Dr. Kalam is a true role model for every Indian. 

	His life teaches us to dream big and work hard with honesty and faith.
